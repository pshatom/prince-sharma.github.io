Projects, Publications, and Collaborations

üîß Ongoing and Previous Projects

  • Design of amorphous complexions in nanocrystalline tungsten alloys for nuclear radiation tolerance, using density functional theory driven discovery of dopants to stabilize amorphous grain boundary complexions (Under leadership of Prof. Timothy J. Rupert, Hopkins Extreme Materials Institute, Johns Hopkins University).
  • Machine learning for predicting elastic constants in binary and complex magnesium compounds, as well as high-entropy Laves phases (2024).
  • Investigation of surface oxidation on high-entropy surfaces (Under leadership of Prof. Ganesh Balasubramanian, University of New Haven, and Prof. TeYu Chen, University of Wyoming, 2024).
  • Maximizing solid solution strengthening in niobium-hafnium-titanium alloys for high-temperature applications (NASA Ames Research Center, 2023).
  • Optimizing the rhenium effect in tungsten alloys for aerospace applications (Collaboration with Dr. William C. Tucker, NASA Ames Research Center, 2023).
  • Engineering phonon transport in disordered two-dimensional MXenes and pnictogen-carbon systems (2023).

Supported by Frontera TACC allocation of over 500,000 node hours (Principal Investigator: Prof. Ganesh Balasubramanian; Allocation Manager: Dr. Prince Sharma).
